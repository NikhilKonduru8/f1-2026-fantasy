FANTASY STRATEGY

POINTS PER MILLION · CHIP TIMING · TRANSFER STRATEGY · 2026 NOTES

📐 The PPM Framework

Points Per Million (PPM) = fantasy points ÷ price. The only metric that matters. Verstappen: 28.0 PPM. Bearman: 27.8 PPM. Russell: 10.5 PPM. You get nearly the same production from a $7.4M Bearman as from a $27.4M Russell — spend the savings on an elite anchor instead.

🏁 The Two Zones Rule

Spend in two zones only: Premium ($25M+) for one elite driver with high ceiling — pick Verstappen or Norris, both PPM >14. And Budget ($3-8M) for value picks like Bearman, Ocon, Hülkenberg. Avoid the $9–23M dead zone entirely.

⚡ Use Your 2× Boost Wisely

Your weekly Boost doubles one driver's score. Always apply it to Verstappen or Norris — they score 50-60+ pts on a good weekend. High-scoring circuits: Monaco, Monza, Silverstone, Suzuka. Never waste it on midfield drivers regardless of form.

🔄 Transfer Strategy

You get 3 free transfers in 2026, with 1 rollover allowed. Save transfers for after 3-4 races when prices update. The biggest gains come from buying drivers before a run of favorable circuits. Don't burn transfers chasing single-race upgrades.

🎰 2026 Regulation Wildcard

2026 is a full regulation reset — new cars, new power units. Mercedes is widely tipped as the PU leader (supplying Mercedes, McLaren, Williams, Aston Martin). Red Bull is running their first in-house Ford PU. Be ready to pivot early if Red Bull struggles.
